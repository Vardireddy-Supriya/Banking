OVERVIEW

Full Name: State Bank of India.


Headquarters: Mumbai, Maharashtra, India.


Founders: SBI as a nationalized entity doesn't have individual founders in the traditional sense, its historical institutions were established by British colonial administrators and private investors. SBI's nationalization in 1955 was spearheaded by the Government of India under the guidance of then-Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru and Finance Minister C.D. Deshmukh, who played a pivotal role in the transformation of the bank into a state-owned entity..


Services: State Bank of India (SBI) offers a wide range of services to its customers across various sectors, including personal banking, corporate banking, and international banking.SBI Bank offers a wide range of banking services, including retail banking, wholesale banking, credit cards, loans, wealth management, and investment products. It caters to individuals, small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and large corporates.


Digital Innovation: State Bank of India (SBI) has been a leader in adopting digital innovations to enhance customer experiences, streamline banking processes, and offer convenient, tech-driven solutions.

ATM and Branch Network: State Bank of India (SBI) has one of the most extensive ATM and branch networks in India and abroad, reflecting its status as the country's largest public sector bank.

International Presence: tate Bank of India (SBI) has a significant international presence, operating across various countries to cater to Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), multinational businesses, and individuals engaged in foreign trade. The international branches and subsidiaries provide a range of services, from retail banking and corporate banking to remittances, trade finance, and foreign exchange.(as representative offices).


Awards and Recognition: State Bank of India (SBI) has received numerous awards and recognitions, including being named the "Best Bank" in India by various financial publications, winning the "Excellence in Digital Banking" award, and earning accolades for its corporate governance, CSR initiatives, and customer service excellence.


Sustainability and CSR: State Bank of India (SBI) is committed to sustainability and corporate social responsibility (CSR) through initiatives focused on green banking, community development, financial literacy, and environmental conservation, including financing renewable energy projects and supporting education and healthcare programs.
